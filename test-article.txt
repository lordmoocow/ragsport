England vs Australia - 5th Ashes Test Match Report

England secured a dramatic victory in the fifth Ashes Test at The Oval, winning by 49 runs to level the series 2-2. Stuart Broad took the final wicket in his last Test match before retirement, dismissing Alex Carey to seal a memorable win.

Joe Root was named Player of the Match after scoring 91 in the first innings and a crucial 78 in the second. His partnership with Chris Woakes proved decisive, adding 155 runs when England were in trouble at 147-5.

The Australian bowling attack, led by Mitchell Starc and Pat Cummins, had reduced England to a precarious position on day three. However, the home side's lower order showed remarkable resilience.

Moeen Ali contributed vital runs lower down the order, and his off-spin proved effective on a wearing pitch. He finished with figures of 3-48 in Australia's second innings.

The series will be remembered for its intensity and drama. Both teams showed exceptional fighting spirit throughout the five matches. The Ashes urn remains with Australia as the holders, but England can take pride in their performance.

Stuart Broad's retirement caps a legendary career spanning 167 Test matches and 604 wickets. He finishes as England's second-highest wicket-taker of all time.

Man of the Series was awarded to Zak Crawley for his explosive batting at the top of the order, including a match-winning century at Old Trafford.
